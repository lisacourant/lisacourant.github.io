My personal website

Front-end web development Evolus Pencil (Mockups) jQuery Gimp Photoshop Skeleton

Here it is, my first personal website! I guess the purpose of this project is obvious, so I will try to go straight to the point. :)

Why this design?

1. "Orange is the new black"

Since the beginning, I wanted my main theme to be all black and orange, with a white writing. Indeed, for some black represents negativity or grief. But for me, black is only elegance and strength, power and sophistication. To enlighten it, I chose to use orange, which is one of my all time favorite colours: orange is vibrant and stimulating, and promotes a sense of joy and creativity. In the end, the combination of those two colours is the perfect match to represent who I want to be!

2. Everything is movement

The choice of creating a very dynamic, moving portfolio might be questionnable. Of course, I could have done a very clean, organised design, but that would not have been me. I wanted something dynamic, yet very easy to understand and follow along. I wanted to have fun creating and developing it, but I didn't want the users to get lost browsing it. That is why I decided to make a single page application, with few sections, clearly organised but always in motion.

How?

I developed this website without using any pre-conceived theme. To do so, I used the CSS library "Animate.css", that helped me realise the animations, and the lightweight CSS framework "Skeleton", to organise my work (particularly the "about" page).

Insectophagia

Human-Computer Interaction Android Java Front-end web development Balsamiq Mockups

Insectophagia is a digital epistemic game: based on a serious game, Insectophagia goes further by combining the use of a digital tool (touch pad) and a game in real-life situation. The principle here is to give high school students the opportunity to carry out a mountain orienteering trail, which will be punctuated with riddles and other activities proposed on the touch pad where Insectophagia is launched. The goal is to improve communication between students and to use modern media to learn while having fun.

When my internship began, Insectophagia was an existing project: some work had already be done on the mobile app. My work was, on the first hand, to finish the implementation of all the functionnalities of Insectophagia, and on the other hand to improve the overall design of the application, making it look more modern and user-friendly.

Another big part of my internship was the creation of a web application that made the customization of the mobile app possible and easy. This way, the teachers can modify the riddles, the map and all the variables of the mobile app without getting involved with any code.

Why?

The JEN.lab project focuses on the design and use of digital epistemic games (DEG) in education and training. This involves co-designing three DEGs (including Insectophagia) that are used in real conditions and disseminated to practice environments. The concerned contexts are those of secondary education and professional training.
